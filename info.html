Primary pollutants

Particulate matter (PM2.5 y PM10)

Solid and liquid particles suspended in the air. Source: Mixture of solid and liquid particles in the air, such as dust, soot, smoke, and metals, originating from cars, factories, and wood burning.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂)

A gas that can irritate the respiratory system, especially in children, and affect lung function. Sources: Combustion of fuels in vehicles, power plants, and industrial facilities.

Formaldehyde (CH₂O / HCHO)

A gas that can be irritating and is found in the air, a colorless gas, may seem irrelevant to many, but its presence in our daily lives poses significant health risks. Source: An essential component of the manufacturing industry, commonly used in the production of various materials, from plastics and textiles to furniture and household products.

Aerosol Index(AI)

Refers to the measurement of the amount of particles and aerosols suspended in the atmosphere, which affect visibility and health. Source: Suspended particles (dust, soot, smoke) come from biomass burning, vehicle traffic, and industrial processes.

Ozono (O₃)

A gas that can irritate the respiratory system and worsen lung diseases. It comes from: Total Volatile Organic Compounds (TVOC) from various sources, mainly solvents and paints, industries, road traffic (mainly two-wheeled vehicles), and plants.
